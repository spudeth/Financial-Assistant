# Rebuild prompt: Gradient background + Chat History drawer + Settings/Profile page

This describes three UI pieces from an existing React/Tailwind web app that need to be rebuilt natively for an Expo (React Native) app. Match the look and behavior described below as closely as the platform allows. Use React Native equivalents (View, Text, Pressable, Animated/Reanimated, expo-blur, expo-linear-gradient, etc.) instead of web/Tailwind classes.

---

## 1. Gradient Background ("GradientBackground")

A full-screen animated-feeling backdrop that sits behind every screen in the app, edge to edge, ignoring safe areas (it should bleed under notches/status bars).

**What it looks like:**
- The base screen color is a very dark, near-black tint (changes per theme — see palette table below; default is `#0d0716`, a near-black purple).
- Three large colored circles ("blobs") are placed behind the content, each heavily blurred (blur radius ~90px) so they look like soft glowing color clouds rather than sharp circles. Each blob is semi-transparent (about 55% opacity).
  - Blob 1: 520x520px, positioned near the top-left, partly off-screen (top: -160, left: -120).
  - Blob 2: 460x460px, positioned on the right side, about 30% down the screen, partly off-screen to the right (right: -160).
  - Blob 3: 560x560px, positioned near the bottom-left, partly off-screen at the bottom (bottom: -200, left: -80).
  - All three blobs are perfect circles (border-radius 50%).
- On top of the three blobs, there's one more full-screen layer: a radial gradient centered at the top of the screen, going from transparent in the middle-top down to the solid base background color toward the edges/bottom. This is what fades the blobs out and keeps the very edges of the screen looking solid/dark rather than colorful — it makes the blobs look like soft glows breaking through the corners rather than fully visible shapes.
- This whole thing sits behind all other UI (z-index below everything) and never receives touches.

**Color themes (5 total, user-selectable):** Each theme swaps the base background and 3 blob colors, plus an "accent" and "accent-2" color used elsewhere in the app for gradients (buttons, the user's avatar, etc.):

| Theme | Base background | Blob 1 | Blob 2 | Blob 3 | Accent | Accent 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Purple (default) | #0d0716 | #6b1f9c | #c026d3 | #4338ca | #a855f7 | #ec4899 |
| Green | #051512 | #047857 | #10b981 | #0f766e | #34d399 | #14b8a6 |
| Blue/Ocean | #051026 | #1e40af | #0ea5e9 | #0369a1 | #38bdf8 | #6366f1 |
| Sunset | #1a0810 | #c2410c | #db2777 | #ea580c | #fb923c | #f43f5e |
| Mono/Charcoal | #0a0a0a | #404040 | #525252 | #262626 | #d4d4d8 | #a1a1aa |

A button/avatar with an "accent gradient" is a 135-degree diagonal linear gradient from `accent` to `accent-2`.

There's also a reusable "glass" card style used throughout the app (settings sheets, profile cards, history menu): translucent white fill (~6% white), blurred backdrop (~28px blur + saturation boost), a thin ~12%-white border, and a soft drop shadow. In Expo, approximate this with `expo-blur`'s BlurView plus a semi-transparent white overlay and border.

## 2. Chat History Drawer ("HistoryDrawer")

A left-side slide-in panel (like a hamburger drawer) that lists past conversations, similar to ChatGPT/Perplexity's history sidebar.

**Trigger/animation:**
- Slides in from the left edge, covering 88% of screen width up to a max width of 380px.
- Slides in/out with a 300ms ease-in-out transform animation.
- A dark backdrop (50% black, slightly blurred) fades in behind it and covers the rest of the screen; tapping the backdrop closes the drawer.
- The panel's own background is the theme's solid base background color (not transparent).

**Layout, top to bottom:**
1. **Header row** (at the very top, respecting top safe area): a circular Settings (gear icon) button on the left, the user's name centered (e.g. "You"), and a circular "close/forward" arrow button on the right that closes the drawer. Both icon buttons are 36x36px circles, dim white icon color by default, lightly highlight on press.
2. **Search bar**: a full-width pill/rounded capsule (rounded-full) with a translucent white background (8% white), a small search icon on the left, and a text input with placeholder "Search threads". Typing filters the thread list below by title or preview text, case-insensitive.
3. **Scrollable thread list** (fills remaining space, no visible scrollbar):
   - Loading state: centered gray text "Loading…" while fetching.
   - Empty state: centered gray text — "No matches." if there's a search query with no results, otherwise "No conversations yet."
   - Each thread row:
     - Bottom border hairline (6% white) separating rows.
     - Title: bold, white, max 2 lines (clamped/truncated with ellipsis if longer).
     - Preview snippet: dimmer white/gray, max ~2 lines, with a gradient fade-to-background overlay at the bottom of the snippet so long text fades out smoothly rather than cutting off hard.
     - Relative timestamp below the preview, small and very dim (e.g. "Just now", "3h ago", "2d ago", or a full date if older than a week).
     - Tapping a row selects/opens that conversation.
     - A "···" (more options) button appears in the top-right corner of each row, only visible on hover (or always visible on touch devices — for Expo, just always show it lightly visible since there's no hover). Tapping it opens a small dropdown/menu anchored under it with a single red "Delete" option (trash icon + text). Tapping outside the menu closes it. Deleting removes the thread from the list immediately and fires a delete request in the background.
4. **Floating "new conversation" button**: a circular button (48x48px) with the accent gradient fill, a white "+" icon, floating in the bottom-right corner of the drawer (above the bottom safe area, ~32px up from bottom, ~16px from right edge). Slight press-down scale animation on tap. Tapping it starts a new conversation and closes the drawer.

**Data behavior:** Thread list is fetched fresh every time the drawer opens (not cached), filtered client-side as the user types in search.

## 3. Settings / Profile Page ("Profile")

A full-screen page that slides in from the right (300ms ease transition) over the gradient background, used for the user's profile/settings.

**Layout, top to bottom:**
1. **Header row**: a circular glass "back" button (chevron-left icon) on the left that closes the page; a glass rounded-square "settings" button (gear icon) on the right that opens a separate bottom Settings Sheet (see below). Both ~40x40px.
2. **Profile header block**, centered: a large rounded-square avatar (80x80px, rounded corners ~24px) filled with the accent gradient, showing the user's initials in white. Below it, the user's display name in a large serif/display font.
3. **Tab bar**: four tabs in a row — "Check-in", "Customize", "Documents", "Account" — separated by thin vertical dividers. Active tab is bold white; inactive tabs are dim white. A thin horizontal line in the accent color runs under the whole tab bar (like an underline indicator, but full width rather than per-tab). Tapping a tab switches the content below.
4. **Tab content** (scrollable):
   - **Check-in tab**: A glass card showing a flame icon (in an accent-gradient square), the user's current streak count ("N day streak") and a small caption "Keep showing up." Below that, another glass card showing a calendar grid for the current month — 7 columns, one cell per day of the month, each a small rounded square. Days the user checked in are filled with the accent gradient and white text; other days are a faint translucent gray. Today's cell gets a white ring/outline around it.
   - **Customize tab**: A "Color theme" section listing all 5 themes as full-width glass rows, each showing 3 small color-swatch circles (the theme's blob colors) plus the theme name. The currently active theme has a white ring/outline around its row. Tapping a row switches the whole app's theme. Below that, a "Message text color" section: a row of small circular color swatches the user can tap to change the color of their own chat message text; the selected one has a white border, others have a faint border.
   - **Documents tab**: A list of glass rows, one per document, showing the document name, its date and file size as a subtitle, and a small circular download button on the right (icon only).
   - **Account tab**: Glass rows showing read-only Name, Email, and Phone fields (label left, value right). Below that, a glass "Change password" row (tappable, currently a placeholder/no-op). Below that, a "Log out" button (translucent white background, logout icon + text) that signs the user out. At the very bottom, small centered dim text showing the app's name.
5. **Settings Sheet** (separate overlay, opened from the header gear icon): A glass panel that slides up and pins to the bottom of the screen with rounded corners (~24px), with a fade-in animation. Contains:
   - Title "Settings" in display font.
   - Toggle row: "Show timestamps" (on/off switch).
   - Toggle row: "Voice replies (TTS)" (on/off switch).
   - A "Voice" row with a dropdown/picker to choose a TTS voice from a list.
   - A small "Notifications" section label, then three toggle rows: "Daily check-in", "Spending insights", "Goal progress".
   - A full-width "Done" button at the bottom with the accent gradient fill that closes the sheet.
   - Tapping outside the sheet (on the dimmed backdrop) also closes it.
   - **Toggle switch style**: a horizontal pill (44x24px). When on, the pill fill is the accent gradient and the small white circular knob sits on the right side; when off, the pill is a faint translucent gray and the knob sits on the left. The knob slides between positions on toggle.

**General visual rules that apply across all three pieces:**
- Display/serif font (EB Garamond or similar) is used only for big headline-style text: greetings, the profile name, sheet titles. Everything else (buttons, labels, body text, chat bubbles) uses a clean sans-serif (Inter or similar).
- Corner radius: big cards/sheets use ~22px rounded corners; buttons/badges that should look like pills use fully rounded (capsule) corners.
- "Glass" surfaces (cards, sheets, menus) are always translucent + blurred + thin light border + soft shadow, as described above — never flat opaque white.
- All interactive icon buttons are circular, ~36-40px, with a subtle background highlight on press/hover.
